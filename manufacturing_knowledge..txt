Hole quality defects may be caused by tool wear, incorrect feed rate, spindle vibration,
fixture misalignment, clamp looseness, burr formation, wrong drill size, material stack-up
variation, or incorrect NC program parameters.

First checks should include tool condition, drill life, fixture alignment, clamp pressure,
hole diameter measurements, surface finish, burr inspection, and comparison against prior
nonconformance records.

Corrective actions may include replacing the tool, recalibrating the fixture, adjusting feed
and speed, inspecting the spindle, reviewing the NC program, and updating work instructions.
